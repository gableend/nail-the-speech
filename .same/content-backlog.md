## Reusable playbook (learned shipping #1)

Things that worked on article #1 that we should keep doing:

- Caption-first clip selection. Videographer/creator captions often describe *why* the speech works — useful to steer the archetype for each example section.
- One named archetype per clip. Easier to remember and share than abstract points.
- Framework section that summarises the clips, not a generic how-to. Reinforces the examples.
- Two common fails at the end. Gives the reader a quick sanity check.
- "Try this" voice-recorder exercise as the closing CTA bridge into `/generator`.

Things to avoid:

- Em dashes. Reads as AI prose. Use periods, commas, colons, parentheses.
- More than 3–5 clips per article. Dilutes the commentary.
- Summarising a speech. Describing *what the best man says* instead of *what the speech is doing*.
- Generic "and then he met her and everything changed" sentimentality.
