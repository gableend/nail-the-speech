# NailTheSpeech Growth Execution Plan

## Objective

Drive more organic traffic and conversions for NailTheSpeech by:

- Publishing high-value editorial content optimized for SEO and conversion.
- Launching a TikTok-based content engine using curated wedding speech clips plus AI-hosted commentary.

The goal is to create a repeatable system where one source of content can feed both the website and social channels.

---

## PHASE 1: On-Site Editorial / SEO Content Engine

### Goal

Create high-intent, SEO-friendly content that:

- ranks on Google
- keeps users engaged with embedded TikTok/social proof
- converts users into trying NailTheSpeech

### Content Strategy

We are **not** simply curating TikToks. We are extracting patterns, insights, and frameworks from viral wedding speech content. Each article must provide original editorial value.

Every article should answer:

- Why does this speech work?
- Why does it fail?
- What structure is being used?
- What emotional arc or comedic device is being used?
- How can the reader apply this in their own speech?

### Article Structure Template

Each article should include:

**1. SEO-first Title**

Examples:

- Best Man Speech Examples That Actually Work (With Real Clips)
- Worst Wedding Speech Fails on TikTok (And How to Avoid Them)
- 5 Viral Wedding Speech Structures You Can Copy

**2. Introduction / Search Intent Match**

Quickly answer the search query and frame the value.

Example:

> "Looking for the best man speech examples that are funny, emotional, and memorable? We analyzed dozens of viral TikTok wedding speeches to find what actually works."

**3. Embedded Clips / Examples**

- Use 3–7 clips per article maximum.
- Do not let embeds dominate the article.
- They support the analysis.

**4. Commentary / Analysis per Example**

For each clip:

- What happens?
- Why it works / fails
- Key takeaway
- How to replicate / avoid

**5. Reusable Framework Section**

Codify patterns into repeatable templates.

Example: The *"Relatable Setup → Twist → Emotional Landing"* framework.

**6. Strong CTA**

Drive users to specific generator pages.

Examples:

- Generate your own best man speech in this style.
- Turn this structure into your own wedding speech.

Link to:

- `/best-man-ai-speech-generator`
- `/maid-of-honor-ai-speech-generator`

### Initial Content Cluster

Produce these first:

1. Best TikTok Best Man Speeches (And Why They Work)
2. Worst Wedding Speech Fails (And How to Avoid Them)
3. 5 Wedding Speech Structures That Go Viral
4. Funny Maid of Honor Speech Examples
5. Emotional Father of the Bride Speeches

### SEO Rules

Ensure each article includes:

- primary keyword in title/H1
- supporting keywords in H2/H3
- 1,500–2,500 words minimum
- internal links to generator pages
- internal links to related articles
- enough original text that the article stands alone without embeds

## PHASE 2: TikTok Content Engine

### Goal

Use viral / interesting wedding speech clips as source material for short-form reaction / analysis content.

The purpose:

- build awareness
- drive traffic
- create backlinks / mentions
- funnel traffic to NailTheSpeech

### Positioning

We are **NOT** a generic wedding speech account.

We are:

> "The account that explains **why** wedding speeches work or fail."

The host should act as a critic / analyst. Not just a narrator.

### Video Structure Template

Each video should follow this structure:

**0–2 sec: Hook**

Examples:

- "This best man speech almost ruined the wedding…"
- "This joke completely saved the speech…"

**3–8 sec: Setup**

Quick context.

**8–20 sec: Clip**

Show only the strongest moment. Trim aggressively.

**20–35 sec: Commentary / Analysis**

Explain:

- why it worked / failed
- what lesson viewers can use

**35–45 sec: CTA**

Examples:

- "Steal this structure with our AI speech writer. Link in bio."

### AI Host / Avatar

We do not want Graeme on camera.

Use AI avatar / AI voice / stylized presenter.

Potential tools:

- HeyGen
- AI voiceovers
- subtitle-driven faceless format

The avatar should feel opinionated and consistent. The value is in the commentary and judgment.

### Video Categories

Create videos around:

- **Wins** — Amazing speeches / emotional moments / funny lines
- **Fails** — Awkward jokes / bad timing / cringe moments
- **Frameworks** — "Why this structure works"
- **Tips** — "How to open a best man speech"

### Weekly Workflow

- **Day 1:** Find 20–30 clips
- **Day 2:** Analyze clips and extract hooks / lessons
- **Day 3:** Write 10–20 scripts
- **Day 4–5:** Batch-produce videos
- **Day 6–7:** Publish and review performance
